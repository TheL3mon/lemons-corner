Realistic Candle

In this project I attempted to create a realisticly moving candle flame.
This was a small addition to the [trailer video] I created for the Escape Room of ITU in early 2023.

The main component is the flame itself, which is then enhanced by smoke created with the Niagara particle system.
The movement itself is created by UV displacement on both axes.

The horizontal movement is created by the distortion of the X plane. I'm applying a sinus-based panning,
which is then influenced by a small randomized vibration to simulate the movement created by light breezes.
To make the flame appear to be "ducking" vertically, I'm once again utilizing panning, but this time on the Y axis.

Since the candle wick itself is usually not moving around when they are lit, and stationary flames are more stable towards their base
I decided to add a gradient-based control to the overall movement. This way, the more further away the flame is from its base, the more instable it appears.
These tricks could also be used on grass or fur, as they have similar structural movement.
