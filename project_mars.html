Procedural Mars Landscape

This project is one of my creations made in World Machine and Unreal Engine.
My plan was to recreate a planet's surface in a stylized way.
I chose Mars, as it's got a nice color palette and its terrain has strong signs of erosion that I wanted to be challenged by.

I started out with a basic Perlin noise pattern, which I adjusted through curves to achieve the barren look of Mars' landscape.
By looking at photographs of the planet's surface, I could see that while it is relatively flat in general, there are hills and mountains that protrude robustly.

The mountains of Mars are full of ridges and talus slopes as well, which I added through erosion and thermal weathering as the next steps.

The lower parts of Mars' landscape are full of vein-like formations created by the former presence of water.
To achieve this look, I created a height-based mask to select the flat landscape between the mountains,
making sure that it bleeds a little bit into the foot of the mountains too, to create a smoother blend between the landforms.

Since the planet's water contained salt before it evaporated, the salt is now covering these lower parts of the landscape.
To achieve this look, I blended in the previously created mask layer with the albedo of the surface, lightening these lower areas in the process.

The final result in World Machine.

Ported over to Unreal Engine, where I added volumetric fog, procedural athmosphere, and post processing effects to render the final look.
